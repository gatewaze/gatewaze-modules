AAIF — Executive Director Weekly March 10, 2026

To the LF Team:

AI, all the time. Last week, I took a 30 minute conversation and turned it into not just a workable but a really good social engagement strategy document. The conversation with a subject matter expert directed the AI and shaped the output. The AI covered the implementation details. What would have been a weeklong process flatted to an hour or so. A super-power combo I am finding — record a conversation about what you want to do and ask Gemini/Claude/ChatGPT to turn it into a specific artifact.

Of course, everyone else is doing it. Which means the AI crawlers are becoming a far more important part of our technology landscape. Rudy Grigar from LF Engineering heard about a new service from AAIF member CloudFlare that automatically renders any HTML site into markdown, making it much easier for AI crawlers to consume. We flipped the switch on the AAIF website and saw over 5,000 requests from crawlers. In the not so distant future, we’ll need to do this for every LF property.

Also a shout out to our own Jonathan Reimer for publishing a slew of Skills for AI tools focused on developer engagement tasks. Eating the dog food, folks!

Lastly, a shout out to all the women of the LF for International Women’s Day. Much respect, gratitude and appreciation for all that you do.

Tech News That Matters

The “Humanization” of AI

The events of the last week felt like something of a turning point. New model releases from Anthropic and OpenAI can replace wider swatches of human work. They’re using more taxing evaluations that simulate human work more closely. Products are also pivoting to resemble how humans effectively work.

Cursor revealed a massive pivot incorporating agentic processes and multi-modal capabilities where agents watch visual outputs rather than just code and logs. A key element: the harness — the framework around an AI model for tool calling and agentic processes — matters as much or more than the models themselves.

Another undercurrent: speed. OpenAI explained that part of ChatGPT 5.4’s improvements are simply faster responses. They added “steerability” — redirect a chain-of-thought midstream and the AI responds nearly immediately.

All of which leads us to Claude CodeSecurity. The Mozilla development team found 14 high-priority vulnerabilities in two weeks using the new tool. They said it would have taken two months without it.
